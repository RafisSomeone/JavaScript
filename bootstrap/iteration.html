Iteration

Looping is controlled by while, do-while and for, which are sometimes classified as iteration statements.
A statement repeats until the controlling Boolean-expression evaluates to false. The form for a while loop is:
while(Boolean-expression)
statement

DO WHILE
FOR



do while

The sole difference between while and do-while is that the statement of the do-while always executes at least once, even if the expression evaluates to false the first time. In a while, if the conditional is false the first time the statement never executes. In practice, do- while is less common than while.

for

A for loop is perhaps the most commonly used form of iteration. This loop performs initialization before the first iteration. Then it performs conditional testing and, at the end of each iteration, some form of “stepping.” Any of the expressions initialization, Boolean-expression or step can be empty.
